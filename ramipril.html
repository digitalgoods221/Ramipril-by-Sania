Ramipril - Drug Monograph

Introduction

Ramipril is an ACE inhibitor used for the treatment of hypertension and reduction of cardiovascular mortality following myocardial infarction in hemodynamically stable patients.

Chemical Formula

C23H32N2O5

Therapeutic Category

  • ACE Inhibitor
  • Antihypertensive Agent
  • Acts on Renin-Angiotensin System

Dosage Forms

  • Oral tablets and capsules
  • Strengths: 1.25mg, 2.5mg, 5mg, 10mg

Indications

  • Hypertension
  • Cardiovascular risk reduction
  • Post-myocardial infarction (MI)
  • Congestive heart failure

Mechanism of Action

Ramipril is a prodrug converted to ramiprilat in the liver. It inhibits ACE, reducing Angiotensin II and increasing bradykinin levels, resulting in vasodilation and reduced aldosterone secretion.

  • Excretion: 60% urine, 40% feces
  • Half-life: 13â€“36 hours depending on dose
  • Clearance: Ramipril 10.7 mL/min, Ramiprilat 126.8 mL/min

Adverse Effects

  • Dry cough, chest pain, dizziness, hyperkalemia
  • Neutropenia, shakiness, impotence

Contraindications

  • Volume depletion
  • Angioedema history
  • Hypotension
  • Hyperkalemia

Pregnancy

Category D: Contraindicated during 2nd and 3rd trimesters due to fetal risk.

Drug Interactions

  • Food: Avoid potassium products. Food doesn't affect absorption.
  • Drugs: Albuterol may reduce effect; NSAIDs may increase renal risk
  • Disease: Monitor in renal artery stenosis

Off-label Uses

  • Diabetic nephropathy
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Prevention of migraines
